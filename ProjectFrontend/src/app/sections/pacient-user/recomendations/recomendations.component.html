Nutrition

Nutrition is key in the treatment against cancer. The patient should follow a balanced diet to improve treatment performance and prevent malnutrition.

  • Eat 4-5 times a day, do not skip any meals.
  • You can stimulate your appetite with small amounts before the main meal.
  • Consider dietary supplementation in consultation with your specialist.
  • Eat fruit and vegetables daily, preferably in-season and local.
  • Provide extra protein with low-fat, sugar-free dairy products.
  • Incorporate cereals, grains, and their derivatives.
  • Consume 2-3 liters of liquids daily.
  • Avoid soy and its derivatives, as well as foods high in salt, sugar, and saturated fats.
  • Prioritize homemade food, avoiding processed foods. Use extra virgin olive oil (EVOO).

Remember not to obsess over food. A balance and variety of nutrition, combined with moderate exercise, is essential.

Physical Activity

Regular exercise strengthens the physical and mental condition of the patient, helping to avoid some side effects of the treatment.

  • Engage in at least 30 minutes of physical activity daily.
  • Avoid physical inactivity and resume your daily activities after the diagnosis.
  • Include aerobic exercises, flexibility, stretching, and resistance exercises.
  • Prefer outdoor activities that promote social interaction.

Infection Prevention

The treatment may decrease the patient's defenses, so it is crucial to take measures to prevent infections.

  • Be alert to trauma or wounds on the body and apply proper hygiene.
  • Keep your environment clean.
  • Wash your hands frequently, and remember this is also important for those around you.

Personal Care

Oral Hygiene

Maintaining good oral hygiene is crucial to avoid oral infections that may complicate the treatment.

  • Attend periodic check-ups.
  • Use gentle hygiene, avoiding toothpaste with irritants like flavorings or alcohol.
  • Maintain daily oral hygiene.

Skin Care

Skin dryness is common due to cancer treatments. Here are some care tips:

  • Use moisturizing products and avoid those containing alcohol or fragrances.
  • Adopt gentle hygiene for your skin.
  • Do not forget to care for your scalp.

Weight Control

Monitor your weight and overall physical condition weekly. Physical changes may indicate treatment inefficiency or disease progression.
